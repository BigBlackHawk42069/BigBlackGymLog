Big Black Backfill digs deep through your Torn training history, from the front to back, and fills your logs with a thick, fully usable timeline right in your browser. It walks your activity log backward in time, one window at a time, rebuilding your training history from today toward the beginning of your account.

Do Not Leave Torn During the Scan

A scan session can take up to a few minutes. Keep the panel open until you see "Full Backfill Completed!" or "Partial Scan Complete! Resume?" When either appears, that session is finished and your progress is saved.

If you accidentally refresh, close, or lose connection early, your backfill progress is retained and you can resume manually when you return. While scanning, this script's other API calls are automatically paused and resume on their own once the scan stops.

Large Logs May Take Several Days

If your history is very large, a single run will hit the row budget before reaching the beginning. That is normal. The scan saves its progress and shows a cooldown only after that budget is actually spent. When the cooldown ends, click Resume and continue where you left off, repeating until the whole log is backfilled.

Partial Scans Are Still Complete

If a run only reaches part of the way back, the data it did retrieve is 100% complete for that span. Backfilled days are only shown once fully populated, so you never see a half-filled day. Just a smaller, fully accurate window that grows each time you resume.

API Info

Torn caps activity-log reads at 50,000 rows per day, shared across every script using the same user ID and API key. Backfill limits itself to 30,000 rows per run, leaving 20,000 rows of headroom for normal sync and other tools. The API also allows roughly 100 calls per minute; backfill paces itself at about one call every 0.7 seconds (~85/min) to stay safely under that limit. This heavy usage only happens during backfill. Once your log is built out, routine sync calls stay lightweight.
